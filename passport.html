        A joint stock company must invest EUR 150,000 and create at least 15 new jobs.

Permanent residence

Foreign nationals are exempt from the requirements of continuous residency for five years and personal financial means equal to the minimum wage if they have invested EUR 1,000,000 or created 100 new jobs. They must also fulfil the following requirements.

        The applicant is covered by public health insurance

        The applicant resides in Romania

        Has command of the Romanian language

        Not a danger to public order or national security


There are no particular guidelines for granting citizenship based on an investor’s residency permit. Investors must go through the standard naturalisation process for becoming Romanian citizens. Foreigners who have lived in Romania as their primary residence for eight years may be given citizenship (or five years, if the foreigner is married and lived with a Romanian citizen).

If you have invested EUR 1,000,000 in Romania, this timescale can be cut in half to just four years, or to two and a half years if the foreign applicant is married to a Romanian national. If the applicant travels overseas for longer than six months in a year, the entire year is ignored for citizenship application.
